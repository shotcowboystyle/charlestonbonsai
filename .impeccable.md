# Impeccable Design Context — Charleston Bonsai

This file captures the design direction for the project. It is read at the start of every Impeccable design skill so that craft, polish, critique, and extract operate from the same foundation.

## Design Context

### Users

The primary audience is **serious bonsai collectors** — people who know species, training styles, soil composition, repotting cycles, and the lineage of pot makers. They evaluate trees on provenance, age, root flare (nebari), branch ramification, and the quality of the photography. Casual gift buyers and beginners are welcome but secondary; the site should never talk down to a collector to court a beginner.

A collector's job-to-be-done on this site:
- Quickly assess whether a tree is worth a deeper look (species, age, size class, current training stage)
- Review specimen-level detail (provenance, training history, current pot, photography from multiple angles)
- Trust that the seller knows the craft as well as they do
- Buy with confidence — or save the tree and come back to it

### Brand Personality

Three words: **confident, hand-tended, exact.**

Voice is that of a craftsperson with decades in the work — opinionated, plainspoken, allergic to marketing language. Trees are described with botanical precision, not "vibes." Charleston is a return address, not a theme.

Emotional goals on first contact:
- *This person knows what they're doing.*
- *Each tree has been seen, not just photographed.*
- *I am being addressed as a peer, not a customer.*

### Aesthetic Direction

**Texture:** Master's atelier rendered in the visual language of **sumi-e ink wash**. The interface should feel like a craftsperson's catalog printed on good paper — bone-white surfaces, soft ink grays, generous negative space (Ma), a single deep accent that earns its presence, brush-stroke or paper-grain texture used sparingly and with intent. The trees are the subject; the UI defers.

**Theme:** Both light and dark are first-class, fully designed modes — not light with a dark afterthought.
- *Light:* Bone / unbleached paper surface with deep sumi-ink text. Reads like an archival specimen catalog.
- *Dark:* Near-black gallery surface with photography lit like sculpture against it. Restraint in color and motion.

**Typography:** Go distinctive — paid or indie faces welcome. The following are banned by default (they read as AI-generated templates): Playfair Display, Fraunces, Cormorant, Crimson, Newsreader, Lora, Inter, DM Sans/Serif, Outfit, Plus Jakarta, Instrument, Space Grotesk, IBM Plex, Syne. Pair a display face with stone-cut or hand-cut presence (look at Sharp Type, Klim, Pangram Pangram, ABC Dinamo, DJR) with a refined humanist body face and an optional mono for specimen-data callouts. The current Playfair + Outfit pairing is to be replaced.

**Color palette:** Monochromatic ink wash plus one accent.
- Surfaces: bone / unbleached paper (light) and near-black ink (dark), both tinted faintly toward the accent hue.
- Text: deep sumi black on light, off-white on dark. Pure #000 and #fff are forbidden — always tinted.
- Accent: **mossy stone-green** — a deep, desaturated foliage / patinated-bronze hue (OKLCH hue ~145, chroma ~0.055 in light, ~0.060 in dark). Locked in during the homepage craft pass. Used sparingly — ≤10% of visual weight. Never as a body-text fill, never as a gradient, never as a background wash.
- Existing Tailwind palette (cream / sage / forest / bark / moss / stone / charcoal) is a *starting point for sampling*, not a constraint. Most of those colors will not survive the reset.

**Anti-references — the site must NOT look like:**
- Generic Etsy/Shopify garden shop (pastels, script type, leafy SVGs, "shop our collection")
- AI-design clichés (glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, sparkle icons, gradient text, side-stripe cards)
- Corporate SaaS dashboard (Inter everywhere, flat geometric icons, hero metric cards, generic "modern minimal")
- Charleston / Lowcountry tourism kitsch (Spanish moss, palmettos, sweetgrass — place is incidental, not a theme)

**Photography direction:** Trees should be shot or presented against neutral, ungimmicky backgrounds. Multiple angles. Scale references where relevant. The site's restraint is in service of giving the photography the entire stage.

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the voice.** Negative space is content. If something can be removed without losing meaning, remove it. Generous Ma between sections, around photography, and inside type blocks.

2. **Density when earned.** Collectors want specs — species, age, height, current pot, training history, provenance. Specimen-data should be dense, well-labeled, and legible without flattening into a spreadsheet. Treat data with the same typographic care as the headlines.

3. **Hand over template.** Visible craft beats clean grids. Intentional asymmetry, subtle paper or ink texture, hand-set type details (real small caps, oldstyle figures, optical sizes). Nothing should feel system-generated.

4. **One ink, then silence.** Monochromatic body with a single accent that earns its place. No second decorative color, no gradient text, no glow effects, no side-stripe cards. Color is rationed.

5. **Motion as breath.** Use motion to acknowledge state changes (entrance, focus, loading) — not to perform. Easing is exponential and quiet. Under `prefers-reduced-motion`, motion fully degrades to instant state changes — not a half-disabled compromise.

### Accessibility Commitments

- **WCAG AAA where feasible** — push to 7:1 for body text. Where the atelier aesthetic genuinely requires a softer contrast (e.g., a paper-grain quote on bone background), document the exception and ensure it does not apply to functional UI.
- **Reduced motion is a full path, not a feature flag.** All GSAP scroll triggers, parallax, and animated reveals must have a `prefers-reduced-motion` branch that disables motion completely. Content must be visible by default; animation only enhances.
- **Keyboard and screen-reader parity.** Gallery filters, listing cards, image viewer, 3D viewer (Three.js), and admin forms must be fully operable without a mouse. The 3D viewer in particular needs a non-visual alternative (multi-angle stills + descriptive alt text) since rotating a 3D model with assistive tech is not realistic.

### Tech Constraints Already in Place

- Nuxt 3 (SSR + SSG), TailwindCSS, GSAP for motion, Three.js for 3D, Pinia, Supabase.
- The Tailwind config currently encodes the old direction (cream/sage/forest palette, Playfair + Outfit, soft/warm/dramatic shadows). This will be revised when the reset lands — until then, treat the current tokens as legacy.
